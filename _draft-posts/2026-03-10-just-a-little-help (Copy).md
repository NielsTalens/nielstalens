Preparation is half the work.
Whether we talk about painting, writing software and in this case providing users with a workplace as a product.

Providing people with a laptop and the possibility to install software just isn't enough.
The goal of an Workplace as a product is that employees can do their jobs on day one.

# Before we start
Before we start building any product we have to have quite some information like: what are the desired outcomes of the product, who are our target users and what problems are we solving?

Prior to that we have to make sure that our product has a valid business case which on its turn is derived from strategic goals. 
But this is another topic.

# Preparing
Know your users.
In the case of a workplace there are a couple of profiles that can be defined for any company.
You have people who do administrative work and some do more operational kind of work.
We have software developers and projectmanagers.

For each group a certain profile could be defined.
We know, and if not we should find out, all that a person with a profile needs.
Again, it is not just making sure that they have the means to install software.
It is more than that.

## Building the profiles
Building the profile by truly understanding the users is where a lot of the value is.
While building the profile we need to understand what their jobs-to-be-done are.
